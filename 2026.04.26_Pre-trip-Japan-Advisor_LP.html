THE PROBLEM

Planning Japan shouldn't feel like a second job.

But for most first-time visitors, it does — and the trip you end up taking is the trip everyone else already took.

01

Information overload

You've spent hours on Reddit, YouTube, and travel blogs. 47 tabs open. You still don't have a plan — just a growing pile of half-confident bookmarks.

02

The language wall

The best izakaya doesn't have an online booking page. The ryokan you saw on Instagram won't show up in English search. Even Google Translate folds when the menu is handwritten on a wall.

03

The same trip everyone else took

ChatGPT gives you a top-10 list. So does every travel blog. So you end up at the same temple as the tour bus that left 30 minutes before you — same selfie spot, same souvenir shop, same trip everyone else just took.

What if your Japan trip didn't have to look like everyone else's?
